Throughout my highschool career, I've been searching for a career that best suits me. I've been exploring with courses that I might be interested in and might pursue in the future. As a freshman, I took the Computer Science Engineering course because one of my hobbies was video games and Computer Science involved working with computers. But after taking the course, I knew it wasn't for me. Sitting all day in a computer lab writing lines of code wasn't very appealing. When choosing classes for the following year, I looked at the course Principles of Engineering and thought how interesting the class could be for me. I asked a few of my friends who took the class that year and they all seemed to enjoy it. What interested me about Principles of Engineering was that it didn't focus on one specific type of engineering. Throughout the year, we focus on all types of engineering from structural to electrical to even computer engineering. From this, I knew that I wanted to take this class.

While in Principles of Engineering, every month or so, we would have a guest speaker in to talk about their profession. There would be many different interesting people coming to talk about what they did and how they performed their jobs. Well, one person that really stuck out to me this year was Aaron Pomerantz. He was an entomologist but not only was he an entomologist, he was also a field biologist. This specific guest speaker stuck out to me because he was relatable, he was just like me. When he was in college and looking for a field of study, he made sure that 1) it was within his interests and 2) was interactive. This guest speaker talked about how he couldn't stay in labs all day and wanted to go around and explore. He would study insects and would regularly find himself out in nature interacting with the environment and habitat. This guest speaker was influential because being a field biologist never really crossed my mind. But after listening to his presentation, I realized that working out in the field and actually interacting with things was perfect for me.

Also, while in Principlse of Engineering, every once and a while, we are given the opportunity to go on field trips to different companies that focus on some sort of Engineering. One place that I went to was Cal Maritime Academy and SunPower. From this field trip, I found that many things were of interest to me. At Cal Maritime, students would work on one big ship. This ship was student-run meaning that no adults or professors were interfering with the ship. This very much interested me because as I stated before, I like being interactive and hands-on with my work. This project at Cal Maritime with the ship fits in perfectly with these character traits that I have. If I were to go there, I could first-hand know how to work on a ship and deal with real life problems that occur while working in the field of engineering. Also, I like how no adults interfere with the work on the ship because it makes the students accountable to make sure the ship is still running. Lastly when we went to SunPower, I found it interesting dealing with solar energy. Especially in today's world, looking for efficient forms of energy is vital in making sure that our world keeps evolving and developing. One of these ways is with solar power. Solar energy comes straight from the sun so as long as the sun is still here, there will always be solar energy. This interested me because working with real-life problems is something that I always find interesting whether its volunteering at a food bank or picking up trash at the beach. There will always be problems in our environment, and sources of energy is something that I could explore interests in when choosing my career.
