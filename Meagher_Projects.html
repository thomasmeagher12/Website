Project: MIT AppInventor Project

Project Name: GeoGuesser

Duration: September 19th - October 6th 2017

Course: Computer Science/Software Engineering

The objective of this project was to make a game using MIT AppInventor. My partner and I created a game where you would study a map of a location and answer questions that would relate to the map. As a team, each day we would assign a task to complete for the night. During the first week of the project, I was the one who was in charge of the project notebook. During the second week of the project, I would expand on our code and add more questions to our game. Throughout the process of making the game, our team ran into some obstacles. One obstacle we ran into was we did not know how to flip screens from the map to the question. We tried many different methods from shaking, to using a button, and using a timer, but we finally figured out a solution. Instead of adding anything to the game, we used the back button on the bottom bezel of the tablet. From this experience, we learned that when a code is not working the way we want, do not give up on your idea. We learned to think of different ways to solve the solution and to think outside the box. You can look at our project notebook at this link.

Project: 1.3.2 Project

Project Name: Solar Hydrogen Vehicle

Duration: October 31st - November 16th 2018

Course: Principles of Engineering

The objective of this project was to design a vehicle from scratch. This vehicle had to work under different power source configurations that include solar and hydrogen cells. As a team, each day we would assign jobs to different people in the group so we could finish our project on time. If someone were to have a bigger role for the group one day, the next day they would let others take lead. We did this, so each person in the group was able to experience and work on different parts of the project and to make sure everyone did the same amount of work. Throughout the beginning stages of our project, we had to brainstorm and construct our vehicle. For my job throughout the first week, I had to create a series circuit for two hydrogen cells and had to think of ways to stabilize our breadboard and our power sources to our vehicle. As well as those specific jobs, I helped construct and build our vehicle. Once we finished constructing our vehicle, we had to test and make sure our vehicle was running in the second week. During this time, my job was to charge the hydrogen cells for testing and brainstorming ideas with our gear ratios, whether it be with our chain and sprocket system or our gears to make our vehicle run faster and smoother. From this project, we learned to manage our time efficiently and to focus on a plan and run with it. Instead of questioning our design from our brainstorming sessions, we focused on one idea and made sure to work with it and roll with it. Also we learned to manage our time efficiently by assigning roles each day of equal work to get our project done on time. You can look at our project notebook at this link.

Project: 2.3.2 Project

Project Name: West Point Bridge Desgin

Duration: March 7th - March 18th 2019

Course: Principles of Engineering

The objective of this project was to create cost-effective, stable bridge that spans the length of the Trinity River to allow people and trucks to cross. My partner and I created four bridge designs that would meet the constraints of the project. Some of these constraints included the cost must be under $300,000, the bridge deck elevation must be between 12 and 24 meters, and members had to be made of carbon steel, high-strength low alloy steel, or quenched and tempered steel. Everyday we would add or improve to our two bridge designs to fit these constraints while also being stable. Once we completed our four bridge designs, we decided on further improving on only one of those. Together, we would add improvements to make the most stable bridge while keeping the cost under the given amount. My job throughout the project varied. For the first week of the project, I was working on and creating two bridge designs, while my partner worked on two designs himself. From there we decided on one of my partners designs. When this happened, he mainly made modifications to the design while I would add some improvements as well. But my main job during this time, was to keep up with the documentation. If you would like to view the documentation, click this link. In the documentation, I focused on writing the description for the brainstorm sketches, the design brief, and any other task related to the documentation. From this project, I learned how to work efficiently during a limited amount of time. We only had eleven days to finish this project and each day we had to focus to not waste any time. Being given the same task as my partner in creating two bridge designs, also helped me be accountable. My partner was there to make sure I was doing my work, while I was making sure he was doing his. This project helped further my knowledge in structural engineering and skills with working as a team.
